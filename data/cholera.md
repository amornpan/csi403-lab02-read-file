# Cholera

## Overview

Cholera is an acute diarrheal infection caused by ingestion of food or water 
contaminated with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae.

## Symptoms

Cholera symptoms can range from mild to severe:
- Profuse watery diarrhea (rice-water stools)
- Vomiting
- Rapid dehydration
- Muscle cramps
- Low blood pressure
- Rapid heart rate

## Transmission

Cholera is transmitted through:
- Contaminated water
- Contaminated food
- Poor sanitation
- Contact with infected feces

## Treatment

Treatment focuses on rehydration:
- Oral rehydration salts (ORS)
- Intravenous fluids for severe cases
- Antibiotics may shorten duration
- Zinc supplements for children

## Prevention

Prevention measures include:
- Safe drinking water
- Proper sanitation
- Hand washing with soap
- Safe food preparation
- Oral cholera vaccine

## Risk Factors

Higher risk in areas with:
- Poor water quality
- Inadequate sanitation
- Overcrowded conditions
- Natural disasters
