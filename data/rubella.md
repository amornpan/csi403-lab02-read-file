# Rubella (German Measles)

## Overview

Rubella, also known as German measles or three-day measles, is a contagious viral infection. 
It is caused by the Rubella virus from the Togaviridae family.

## Symptoms

The main symptoms of rubella include:
- Low-grade fever (37.5-38.5 degrees Celsius)
- Pink rash starting from the face
- Swollen lymph nodes behind ears and neck
- Headache and fatigue
- Sore throat
- Runny nose

## Transmission

Rubella spreads through:
- Respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes
- Direct contact with infected secretions
- Mother to baby during pregnancy

## Treatment

There is no specific treatment for rubella. Management includes:
- Rest at home
- Paracetamol for fever
- Calamine lotion for itching
- Plenty of fluids
- Isolation to prevent spread

## Prevention

The best prevention is vaccination:
- MMR vaccine (Measles, Mumps, Rubella)
- First dose at 9-12 months of age
- Second dose at 4-6 years of age

## Complications

Most cases are mild, but complications can include:
- Arthritis (joint pain)
- Encephalitis (brain inflammation)
- Congenital rubella syndrome (if pregnant women are infected)
